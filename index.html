About

I didn’t enter this field through a predefined career plan. My understanding of professionalism was shaped early by direct exposure to operational reality — where brand ideals, hierarchy, and commercial constraints intersect.

Starting from the ground level, I learned how systems function under pressure, and how upstream decisions translate into downstream outcomes.

Building a Structured Business Foundation

My academic training began with Economics and Management at UCLA, forming a rigorous analytical foundation for business decision-making.

Prior to Oxford, I independently relocated to Shanghai and joined Edelman, where frontline public relations work sharpened my understanding of brand systems, organizational dynamics, and real-world execution.

From Global Training to Operational Sensitivity

In 2012, I joined Shiseido as the starting point of my industry career. This experience shaped my sensitivity to retail systems, standardized brand operations, supply rhythm, and in-store execution.

It is also why I can read operational complexity fluently across both luxury beauty and medical aesthetics today.

Advanced Studies & Cross-Cultural Strategy

At Oxford, I pursued advanced studies in Marketing, Management, and Finance, focusing on cross-cultural market strategy.

Through executive and doctoral-level coursework at USC, I further deepened my research into global luxury brand operations and high-end consumer behavior.
